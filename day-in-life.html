A Day in My Life as a UX Designer

Intentional structure, deep work, continuous learning, and being present as a father.

Every day blends empathy-driven design with a focus on wellbeing. I carve dedicated time for research and prototyping, teach and mentor the next generation, enjoy movement and mindful breaks, and close the day with family. This balance keeps me grounded and helps me grow as both a designer and a dad.

Routine Snapshot

The rhythms that keep impact, learning, and family in harmony

Deep Work Windows 2 × 90 mins

Shielded focus blocks in the morning for research synthesis, prototyping, and writing narrative briefs.

Collaboration 3 touchpoints

Stand-up with product squads, stakeholder alignment, and mentorship sessions with emerging designers.

Growth & Reflection 60 min

Daily learning loop across UX research trends, systems thinking, or teaching material refinement.

Family & Wellness Evenings on

Afternoons reserved for school pick-up, playground explorations, cooking dinner, and bedtime stories.

Daily Rhythm

A balance of impact, curiosity, and presence

06:15

Mindful start with family

I begin with a quiet espresso ritual, gratitude journaling, and preparing breakfast with my daughter. Walking her to school keeps us connected before the workday starts.

  • Personal reflection and intention setting
  • Family breakfast playlist & laughter
  • School drop-off walk to stay intentional
08:30

Deep work & discovery

With notifications muted, I synthesize research, map service blueprints, or craft flows in Figma. I favor writing problem narratives to clarify impact before ideating.

  • User research synthesis & journey mapping
  • Prioritized focus using time-boxed sprints
  • Documenting hypotheses in Notion for the squad
11:00

Team syncs & co-creation

Stand-up with product, engineering, and data to unblock decisions. Afternoons include design critiques or customer interviews, often pairing with PMs to refine outcomes.

  • Squad stand-up and async updates
  • Heuristic reviews with QA & researchers
  • Usability testing or storyboarding with PMs
13:00

Recharge & movement

Lunch away from the screen: a walk for fresh air, strength training, or practicing breathing drills. I return energized, ready to coach and make decisive design moves.

  • Movement practice (HIIT or trail run prep)
  • Mindful lunch—usually homemade
  • Quick journaling on insights & decisions
14:00

Mentorship & delivery

Afternoons are for collaborative work: pairing with developers, running design reviews, and mentoring design students. I love translating complex requirements into simple flows.

  • Design QA alongside engineering partners
  • Mentoring Rahnema College cohorts
  • Aligning roadmap milestones with data insights
17:30

Family time & decompression

I close the laptop and switch to dad mode: playground adventures, homework check-ins, or building LEGO worlds. Evenings end with dinner together and device-free wind down.

  • Cooking with family & teaching life skills
  • Bedtime stories and reflection with my daughter
  • Weekly digital sabbath evenings
21:00

Learning loop & journal

After bedtime, I review notes from the day, curate insights, and spend 30 minutes learning—whether that’s service design systems, product analytics, or woodworking techniques.

  • Reading one article or book chapter
  • Updating my growth board & weekly goals
  • Setting tomorrow’s top three outcomes

How I Stay Balanced

Designing with wellbeing in mind

I plan every week with clear outcomes, but also schedule recovery: trail runs, mountaineering drills, coffee chats with peers, and uninterrupted reading hours.

  • Weekly planning ritual with my personal OKRs
  • 90-minute performance & recovery sessions
  • Monthly sabbatical day dedicated to exploration

Fatherhood informs my craft

Being a father sharpens my empathy. I co-create routines with my daughter, involve her in creativity sessions, and learn from her curious lens to design inclusive products.

  • Shared journaling prompts after school
  • Designing playful prototypes for her feedback
  • Weekend maker projects to explore storytelling

Continuous growth systems

Growth is intentional: weekly mentorship hours, quarterly workshops, and regular writing to distill what I learn. This compounds into better decisions for teams and users.

  • Teaching at Rahnema College to reinforce fundamentals
  • Participating in design critiques & communities
  • Quarterly self-retrospectives with trusted peers
